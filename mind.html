box breathing military snipers 1 week
// Training Protocol
  • 01Inhale through nose — 4 seconds. Belly expands, not chest.
  • 02Hold — 4 seconds. Completely still.
  • 03Exhale through mouth — 4 seconds. All air out.

  • 05Do 4 cycles. Works in under 2 minutes. Use before high-pressure moments.

Falling Safely
Injury prevention — judo breakfall
ukemi survival military 2 weeks
// Training Protocol
  • 01On soft surface: sit → tuck chin to chest (prevents head slam).
  • 02Roll backward, slap the ground with both arms at 45°. Arms stay slightly bent — don't lock elbows.
  • 03Practice from sitting 20x. Then from crouching 20x. Then from standing.
  • 04Forward fall: hands out, bent elbows, head turned — NEVER straight arms.
  • 0510 minutes on grass or mat, 3 sessions. Then it's automatic.

Room Reading
Who's in charge, who's threat, where are exits
tactical awareness military social 2–3 weeks
// Training Protocol
  • 01Every time you enter a room: before touching your phone — count exits first.
  • 02Identify the loudest person and the quietest person. Who does others look at when they speak?
  • 03Find who is scanning the room (alert) vs locked into conversations (tunnel vision).
  • 04Note body language clusters: crossed arms, feet pointed away = disengaged. Leaning in = invested.
  • 05Daily: write 3 observations from any room. After 3 weeks, it runs in background automatically.

Distance by Eye
Estimate meters/yards without tools
dead reckoning military navigation 3 weeks
// Training Protocol
  • 01Know your own pace: walk 100 steps. Measure with phone GPS or map. That's your personal pace count.
  • 02Daily: guess a distance (to a building, car, tree). Then walk it and count steps. Check against guess.
  • 03Learn visual anchors: a standard car is ~4.5m, a door is ~2m, a floor height is ~3m.
  • 04Stack anchors for longer distances: "That's about 5 cars away = 22m."
  • 05After 3 weeks of daily guessing: reliable to within 15% without thinking.

Precise Speech
Say exactly what you mean — no excess
signal vs noise military comms leadership 2 weeks
// Training Protocol
  • 01After every message you send (text/voice): read it back. Count filler words. "Basically, kind of, sort of, I think, maybe."
  • 02Rewrite removing every filler. Does the meaning change? It shouldn't. If it does, the filler was hiding uncertainty.
  • 03Military rule: State → Reason → Action. "Enemy at north gate — blocking exit — we move east now."
  • 04Practice with a timer: explain any topic in exactly 30 seconds. No more, no less.
