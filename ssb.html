Assessors assess you across all 3 dimensions simultaneously: The Psychologist assesses Manasa (how you think), the Interviewing Officer assesses Vacha (how you speak/express), and the GTO assesses Karma (what you do under pressure). Consistency across all three is the key to recommendation.

OIR — Officer Intelligence Rating
FormatTime-bound verbal & non-verbal reasoning
TestsLogic puzzles, analogies, series completion, spatial reasoning
GoalAssess baseline analytical ability; not knowledge
PPDT — Picture Perception & Discussion Test
👁 Step 1 — Observation 30 seconds
A hazy picture is shown on screen. Observe carefully.
📝 Step 2 — Note Details 1 minute
Write each character's gender (M/F/P), age, and mood (+/–/0), plus a one-line action statement.
✍️ Step 3 — Story Writing 4 minutes
Write a story covering: what led to this, what is happening, and the likely outcome.
🗣 Step 4 — Group Discussion
Each candidate narrates their story. Group then reaches a common consensus story. No debate — collaborative.
✅ Candidates who clear: receive new chest numbers and proceed to Stage 2.
⏱
Continuous 2.5-hour battery with only one short break. Tests the subconscious mind — no direct observation pressure. Dress: Formals.

🏠 Indoor Tasks (3)
Group Discussion (GD)
2 topics, ~20 min each. Friendly & informal — no need to reach a conclusion. Show knowledge, respect for others' views, and communication.
Group Planning Exercise (GPE)
Observe a model (map) → Read narrative with intertwined problems (5 min) → Write individual solution (10 min) → Group discussion & final solution (20 min).
Lecturette
Pick a card with 4 topics → choose one → 3 min prep → deliver a 3-min speech to the group. Tests confidence & knowledge.
🌳 Outdoor Tasks (6)
Progressive Group Task (PGT)4 progressively harder obstacles using planks, ropes & loads. All cross together.
Half Group Task (HGT)PGT-style in a smaller subgroup. Single obstacle, 15 minutes.
Snake Race (Group Obstacle Race)Highly physical race over 6 obstacles while collectively carrying a heavy rolled-up tent ("the snake").
Individual Obstacles (IO)10 obstacles numbered 1–10 by points. Complete in 3.5 minutes. Finish all? Repeat for extra points.
Command TaskYou are commander for 15 min. Choose 2 members who follow your exact orders (no talking/questioning) to complete an obstacle.
Final Group Task (FGT)PGT-style. Full group reunites. One final mission together — the closing assessment.
PGT — The 5 Rules (Critical to Know!)
GROUP RULE
Everyone must cross together. No one is left behind.
DISTANCE RULE
Gaps >4 ft must be bridged with materials.
COLOUR RULE
White = all can touch. Blue = men only. Red = out of bounds.
RIGIDITY RULE
Materials cannot be tied together permanently.
INFINITY RULE
Start & finish lines extend endlessly in both directions.

Strike Corps vs Holding Corps — Key Distinction
⚔️ Strike Corps
Offensive

Mission: Exploit gaps created by Holding Corps, launch deep thrusts into enemy territory, encircle and destroy enemy forces.

  • Equipped with Armoured and Mechanised formations
  • Supported by self-propelled artillery (K9 Vajra)
  • High mobility — designed to advance rapidly
  • India has 3 Strike Corps (I, II, XXI Corps)
🛡 Holding Corps
Defensive

Mission: Hold current positions, absorb enemy attack, prevent breakthrough, and create conditions for Strike Corps to exploit.

  • Stronger in Infantry and Artillery
  • Occupies defensive positions along borders
  • Attrits the enemy before Strike Corps is unleashed
  • India has 4 Holding Corps
Theaterisation — Hot SSB Topic
⚔️
What is Theaterisation?
Ongoing reform to integrate Army, Navy, and Air Force under unified "Theatre Commands" with a single operational commander. Aim: eliminate duplication, improve joint warfare. Currently India has 2 operational joint/tri-service commands:
Andaman & Nicobar Command (ANC)
India's first tri-service command; covers the strategic island chain
Strategic Forces Command (SFC)
Manages India's nuclear arsenal; ensures credible minimum deterrence
📌 Theatre Commands (Air Defence, Maritime, Land) are under discussion and not yet operationalised. The Chief of Defence Staff (CDS) is the principal military advisor facilitating this integration.
Key Wars India Has Fought
First war after Independence over Jammu & Kashmir. Led to the UN-mandated ceasefire with Pakistan retaining part of J&K (PoK). The Instrument of Accession signed by Maharaja Hari Singh brought J&K into India.
War with China over disputed border. India suffered significant losses. Led to major military reforms — raised mountain divisions, improved logistics, and established Forward Policy. Key outcome: awakening of India's need for defence preparedness.
17-day war; ended in stalemate with Tashkent Agreement. Battle of Asal Uttar — India's largest tank battle, where T-55s of Pakistan were repulsed. IAF played a significant role in ground support.
India's greatest military victory. 93,000 Pakistani soldiers surrendered — the largest since World War II. Led by Field Marshal Sam Manekshaw. Navy blocked sea lanes, IAF achieved air superiority, Army conducted rapid operations. Led to creation of Bangladesh.
Pakistani soldiers & militants occupied peaks in Kargil/LOC. India launched Op Vijay to recapture them. IAF launched Op Safed Sagar. Bofors howitzers proved decisive. All occupied posts recaptured by July 26 — now celebrated as Kargil Vijay Diwas. India suffered 527 casualties but achieved a clear military victory.

📜 Key Policies — Interview Topics
4-year short-service tenure for soldiers (Agniveers). 25% are retained as regular cadre; remaining 75% are released with a corpus of ~₹11.7 lakh (Seva Nidhi) and given priority in state police/CAPFs. Aim: reduce pension burden, bring younger profile to armed forces.
No First Use (NFU) — India will never use nuclear weapons first. Credible Minimum Deterrence — India maintains the smallest arsenal needed to deter adversaries. Massive retaliation — If India or Indian forces are attacked with nuclear weapons, India reserves the right to respond with massive nuclear retaliation. Triad: Land (Agni), Air (Mirage/Rafale), Sea (SSBN).
Goal of reducing imports and building indigenous defence capability. Two positive indigenisation lists — over 300 items banned from import. HAL's Tejas LCA, LCH Prachand, DRDO's Akash SAM, and BrahMos are key examples.
